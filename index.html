Kendrick Lamar

A Legacy of Excellence

Kendrick Lamar's profound impact comes from his masterful blend of lyricism, storytelling, and social commentary, tackling race, identity, and oppression with raw authenticity. His innovation and cultural influence make him a generational voice in hip-hop.

2011 – Release of Section.80

Kendrick Lamar released his debut studio album Section.80, which gained critical acclaim and showcased his storytelling ability. The album was independently released and laid the foundation for his rise in hip-hop.

2012 – Breakthrough with good kid, m.A.A.d city

His second studio album, good kid, m.A.A.d city, was released under Top Dawg Entertainment, Aftermath, and Interscope Records. The album was a commercial & critical success, earning multi-platinum status & being hailed as a modern classic.

2013 – First Grammy Nominations

Lamar received his first Grammy nominations, including Album of the Year for good kid, m.A.A.d city, marking his arrival as a mainstream artist. Although he didn’t win, it solidified his status in the industry.

2015 – Release of To Pimp a Butterfly

Kendrick dropped To Pimp a Butterfly, a jazz and funk-infused album addressing racial and social issues. The album debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 and won Best Rap Album at the 2016 Grammys.

2016 – Wins Five Grammy Awards

At the 58th Grammy Awards, Kendrick won five awards, including Best Rap Album for To Pimp a Butterfly and Best Rap Performance for "Alright," which became an anthem for the Black Lives Matter movement.

2017 – Release of DAMN. and Pulitzer Prize Win

His fourth studio album, DAMN., was released and quickly went triple platinum. In 2018, he became the first non-classical and non-jazz artist to win the Pulitzer Prize for Music for DAMN.

2018 – Curating Black Panther: The Album

Kendrick curated and produced Black Panther: The Album, the soundtrack for Marvel’s Black Panther. The album included hit songs like "All the Stars" and "King’s Dead," earning multiple Grammy nominations.

2022 – Release of Mr. Morale & The Big Steppers

After a five-year hiatus, Kendrick returned with Mr. Morale & The Big Steppers, an introspective and experimental album that won Best Rap Album at the 2023 Grammys.

2024 – Asked to Perform at the Super Bowl for the 2nd Time

Kendrick was invited to perform at the Super Bowl halftime show for the second time, a testament to his cultural impact and electrifying stage presence.

2025 – Wins Five Grammy Awards for "Not Like Us"

Kendrick Lamar won five Grammy Awards for his hit song "Not Like Us," further cementing his dominance in the rap industry and proving his continued influence in modern hip-hop.
